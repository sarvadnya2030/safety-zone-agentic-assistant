India Flood Inventory — District-level risk summary (NRSC/ISRO data).

The India Flood Inventory (IFI) is a national dataset compiled by NRSC (National Remote Sensing Centre) and ISRO using satellite imagery from 1998 onwards.

High flood-frequency districts (historical):
- Assam: Dhubri, Barpeta, Kamrup, Dibrugarh — annual monsoon flooding
- Bihar: Darbhanga, Muzaffarpur, Sitamarhi, Supaul — Kosi and Gandak river flooding
- West Bengal: Murshidabad, Nadia, Malda — Ganga flooding
- Uttar Pradesh: Bahraich, Lakhimpur Kheri — Himalayan river flooding
- Odisha: Kendrapara, Cuttack, Jagatsinghpur — river and coastal flooding
- Andhra Pradesh: East Godavari, West Godavari, Krishna — delta flooding
- Kerala: Ernakulam, Thrissur, Pathanamthitta — 2018 catastrophic floods
- Maharashtra: Kolhapur, Sangli, Raigad — Krishna and Konkan coastal flooding

Flood causes:
- Monsoon intensification (June–September)
- Himalayan river snowmelt (March–May)
- Cyclone storm surge (October–November, east coast)
- Urban flooding from drainage failures

Districts with highest flood frequency score (>0.7): Dhubri (Assam), Darbhanga (Bihar), Murshidabad (WB), Kendrapara (Odisha).
