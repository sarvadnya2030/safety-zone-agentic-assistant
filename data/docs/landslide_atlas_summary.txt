India Landslide Atlas — Frequency and Susceptibility by State/District (NRSC/ISRO).

The National Landslide Atlas of India (2023) was prepared by NRSC using satellite data covering 1998–2022.

Key findings:
- Over 80,000 landslides recorded in 17 states over 24 years
- Top 5 states by event count: Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Kerala, Arunachal Pradesh

Very high susceptibility districts:
- Uttarakhand: Chamoli, Rudraprayag, Pithoragarh, Uttarkashi
- Himachal Pradesh: Kinnaur, Kullu, Lahaul & Spiti, Mandi
- Jammu & Kashmir: Ramban, Doda, Kishtwar
- Kerala: Idukki, Wayanad, Palakkad
- West Bengal: Darjeeling, Kalimpong
- Sikkim: North Sikkim, East Sikkim
- Arunachal Pradesh: Tawang, West Kameng

Monsoon correlation: 70% of landslides in India occur during June–September monsoon. Rainfall >100mm/day is a key trigger.

Road corridors affected: NH7 (Manali-Leh), NH58 (Rishikesh-Badrinath), NH22 (Shimla-Tibetan border).
